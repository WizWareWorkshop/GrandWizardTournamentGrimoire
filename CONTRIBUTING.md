# Contributing to the Grimoire Plugin

Thank you for taking an interest in the Grimoire Plugin.

This repository is public because the spell system behind Grand Wizard Tournament is intended to be understandable, discussable, and reviewable. At the same time, this is a curated project with a specific design direction, and contributions are considered selectively.

Please read `LICENSE.md` before submitting anything.

## What kind of contributions are useful

The most useful contributions are usually:

- structured spell proposals,
- design feedback,
- graph workflow suggestions,
- architecture discussion,
- bug reports,
- clarity improvements,
- and focused technical observations.

In the current phase, thoughtful and well-explained submissions are generally more useful than large unsolicited rewrites.

## What this repository is not

This repository is not an unrestricted open-source collaboration space.

A public repository does not mean that all pull requests will be merged or that outside work automatically becomes official project content. The project owner retains full discretion over what fits the direction of the plugin and the game.

## Before submitting anything

Before opening an issue, discussion, or pull request, please make sure that:

1. your submission is your own work or material you have the right to submit,
2. you are not including third-party code, assets, designs, or text that you do not have permission to share,
3. your submission is specific and understandable,
4. and you are comfortable with the possibility that your contribution may be rejected, ignored, postponed, publicly discussed, or reworked internally.

## Spell ideas

If you want to propose a spell, explain more than the fantasy.

A strong spell submission should describe:
- what the spell is meant to do,
- how it begins,
- how its logic flows,
- which conditions or branches matter,
- which variables matter,
- what the outputs are,
- and why the spell is interesting inside a Grimoire-based system.

A spell idea becomes much more valuable when it can be reasoned about structurally.

## Pull requests

If you open a pull request:

- keep the scope narrow,
- explain the purpose clearly,
- describe the intended behavior,
- and explain why the change fits the project direction.

Large unsolicited refactors, broad architectural rewrites, or changes that move the project away from its intended direction are unlikely to be accepted.

## Issues and discussions

When reporting a problem or starting a discussion, include as much clarity as possible.

Useful reports usually explain:
- what you expected,
- what happened instead,
- how the issue can be reproduced,
- what part of the system is affected,
- and why the issue matters.

## Review and acceptance

All submissions are reviewed at the project owner’s discretion.

A submission may be:
- accepted,
- rejected,
- partially adopted,
- used only as inspiration,
- postponed,
- or reworked internally.

No contribution process described here creates any obligation to merge, publish, adopt, credit, or compensate a submission.

## Additional terms for official inclusion

If any outside contribution is ever considered for official inclusion in the plugin or the game, additional written permission or a separate contributor agreement may be required before acceptance.

## Conduct

Be clear, constructive, and respectful.

The most helpful contributions improve understanding rather than simply increasing volume.
